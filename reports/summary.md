Telco Customer Churn — Analytical Findings Report
	1.	Overall Churn and Revenue Exposure

	•	Total customers analyzed: 7043
	•	Overall churn rate: 26.5 percent
	•	Revenue at risk: 30.5 percent

Conclusion: Revenue exposure is higher than customer churn proportion, indicating financially concentrated churn.

⸻

	2.	High-Churn Customer Characteristics

The following customer segments show significantly higher churn:
	•	Tenure 0–6 months → 52.9 percent churn
	•	Month-to-month contracts → 42.7 percent churn
	•	Electronic check payment users → 45 percent churn
	•	Fiber optic users → 41.9 percent churn
	•	Senior citizens → 41.7 percent churn

Conclusion: Early lifecycle customers and flexible contracts drive instability.

⸻

	3.	Extreme Risk Cluster

Combination identified:

Month-to-month + Fiber optic
	•	Churn rate: 54.6 percent
	•	Monthly churned revenue from this segment: over 100,000

Conclusion: This segment represents the most financially vulnerable cluster.

⸻

	4.	Risk Scoring Framework Results

A 5-factor rule-based scoring model was created.

Risk segments:

Low Risk
Medium Risk
High Risk

Observed churn rates:
	•	Low Risk → 6.6 percent
	•	Medium Risk → 37.6 percent
	•	High Risk → 67.8 percent

Conclusion: Risk score effectively separates stable and unstable customers.

⸻

	5.	Financial Prioritization of Drivers

Revenue-weighted priority ranking:
	1.	Month-to-month contracts
	2.	Fiber optic users
	3.	Electronic check users
	4.	Early tenure customers
	5.	Senior citizens

Conclusion: Financial scale and churn rate together determine strategic priority.

⸻

	6.	Revenue Recovery Simulation

Estimated annual recovery under churn reduction scenarios:

10 percent reduction → 282,176
20 percent reduction → 564,353
30 percent reduction → 846,529
40 percent reduction → 1,128,706

Conclusion: Revenue recovery scales linearly with churn reduction.

⸻

	7.	Break-Even Retention Budget

Target segment size: 3730 customers
Allowable annual retention cost per customer (20 percent improvement case): 151

Conclusion: Retention spending above this level reduces profitability.

⸻

	8.	Strategic Insight Summary

	•	Churn is structurally concentrated, not random.
	•	Early lifecycle and flexible contracts drive instability.
	•	High-value internet plans increase financial exposure.
	•	Retention ROI is measurable and predictable.
